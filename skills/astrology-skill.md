# Astro-Numerology Guide
## A Seven-Voice Synthesis

This skill produces integrated readings that treat astrology and numerology not as
fortune-telling, but as maps of the **psyche in time and eternity** — tools for
individuation (Jung), soul development (Cayce), humanistic unfoldment (Rudhyar),
psychological precision (Greene), ontological number wisdom (Pythagoras), karmic
biography (Steiner), and compassionate spiritual orientation (Hickey).

---

## The Seven Frameworks

### 1. Edgar Cayce — The Soul's Chosen Curriculum
Cayce read the horoscope as a record of **soul memory** — planetary influences reflect
karmic tendencies the soul *chose* before incarnation.
- Planets represent **urges from past-life experiences** in those planetary spheres
- The soul selects its birth moment and chart as a curriculum for this life
- Afflictions are not punishments but **growth edges** from prior patterns
- The physical, mental, and spiritual bodies are unified — a chart reading addresses all three
- Numerology: name and birth date vibrations are **soul signatures chosen before birth**
- Free will always supersedes astrological tendency — "the stars impel, they do not compel"
- Key terms: *sojourn, urge, attunement, akashic record, soul development*

### 2. Carl Jung — The Psyche's Archetypal Drama
Jung saw astrology as a **synchronistic symbolic system** — the birth moment mirrors
the psyche's structure at incarnation.
- Planets and signs map to **archetypes** in the collective unconscious
- The chart reveals the **individuation path** — the journey toward wholeness
- The Shadow (8th house, Pluto, Saturn afflictions) holds denied and undeveloped material
- The **Persona** (Ascendant) vs. the **True Self** (Sun, the whole chart as the Self)
- Anima/Animus dynamics live in Venus/Mars and the 7th house
- Synchronicity: the chart doesn't *cause* — it *coincides with* the soul's pattern
- Key terms: *shadow, individuation, projection, archetype, anima/animus, the Self*

### 3. Dane Rudhyar — The Seed Pattern and Humanistic Unfoldment
Rudhyar synthesized Jung with astrology and founded **humanistic astrology** — the chart
as a seed pattern holding the soul's entire potential at the moment of first breath.
- The birth chart is a **mandala of the whole person** — not a collection of parts but
  an integrated organic unity; interpret holistically before analyzing pieces
- Every placement is a **functional part of one living pattern** — there are no bad planets,
  only energies seeking their right role in the whole
- The **Lunation Cycle** (Sun-Moon phase at birth) reveals the soul's fundamental orientation:
  - New Moon types: instinctive, subjective, pioneering — operating by inner knowing
  - Crescent: overcoming the past, building something new against resistance
  - First Quarter: crisis of action, building structures to express the new
  - Gibbous: self-improvement, striving toward perfection and refinement
  - Full Moon types: objective, relationship-oriented, fulfillment through others
  - Disseminating: spreading ideas, teaching, sharing what was gained at the Full
  - Last Quarter: crisis of consciousness, reformulating values and worldview
  - Balsamic: completing a cycle, prophetic, seed-bearer for future cycles
- **Sabian Symbols**: each of the 360 degrees of the zodiac carries a symbolic image —
  use the degree symbol for the Sun, Moon, Ascendant, and prominently aspected planets
  to add a mythic image layer that bypasses the analytical mind
- The **phase relationship** between any two planets carries meaning equal to the aspect angle
- Key terms: *seed pattern, cyclic unfoldment, phase relationship, holistic chart, mandala*

### 4. Liz Greene — Jungian Astrology in Practice
Greene is the operative method for applying Jung to actual chart interpretation — rigorous,
psychologically precise, and unflinching about difficulty.
- **Saturn** is the most important planet for understanding the psychological task:
  Saturn's sign, house, and aspects reveal the *specific form of the ego's confrontation
  with limitation* — and therefore the specific doorway to individuation
- Relationship astrology as **projection and shadow work**: the 7th house and its ruler
  describe what the person has disowned and seeks in others; conscious relationship work
  means reclaiming those projected qualities
- The **outer planets as fate**: Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto operate as autonomous forces
  that break down ego structures — they cannot be "managed," only met consciously
- **Myth as interpretive layer**: each sign and planet has mythological roots that illuminate
  its deepest meaning; bring in relevant myths where they enrich the reading
  (e.g., Persephone for Pluto, Prometheus for Uranus, Dionysus for Neptune)
- **The family unconscious**: the 4th/10th house axis and parental complexes carry
  inherited unconscious material that shapes the entire chart's expression
- Key terms: *projection, the wound, fate vs. choice, mythological background, Saturn return*

### 5. Pythagoras — Number as Ontological Reality
For Pythagoras, numbers are not symbols *of* reality — they *are* reality. The universe
is constructed from number relationships; numerology is therefore direct perception of
cosmic structure.
- Numbers are **living principles**: 1 is not just "individuality" but the actual principle
  of unity from which all multiplicity proceeds
- The soul resonates with certain frequencies because it *participates* in those cosmic
  principles — not randomly but by nature
- The **Tetractys** (1+2+3+4=10) underlies all interpretation: units (1-4), human
  experience (5-8), completion/return (9-10/1)
- **Harmonic relationships** between core numbers (Life Path, Expression, Soul Urge)
  reveal consonance or discord — treat number combinations like musical intervals
- Master numbers (11, 22, 33) are **unresolved octaves** — the soul holds a higher
  vibration without collapsing it; this is demanding and requires conscious effort
- The **name as sound**: letters combined produce a harmonic chord; the birth name is
  the soul's primary frequency signature in this incarnation

### 6. Rudolf Steiner — Karma as Biography, Biography as Karma
Steiner built the most systematic esoteric account of karma across incarnations, grounding
Cayce's intuitive readings in rigorous spiritual science.
- The chart reflects **karma built across multiple incarnations** — karma is not debt
  but *experience seeking completion and wisdom*
- **Three karmic streams** operate simultaneously:
  - *Individual karma*: what this soul specifically brought and is working through
  - *Group karma*: shared with family, nation, epoch, and spiritual community
  - *Cosmic karma*: the soul's participation in the evolution of consciousness itself
- The **rhythms of seven** — 7-year biographical cycles linked to planetary spheres:
  - 0-7: Moon phase (body formation, foundational imprinting)
  - 7-14: Mercury phase (learning, language, social relationships)
  - 14-21: Venus phase (feeling life, identity formation, beauty)
  - 21-28: Sun phase (individuality emerges, vocation, true self)
  - 28-35: Mars phase (will, achievement, confrontation with the world)
  - 35-42: Jupiter phase (wisdom, expansion, social contribution)
  - 42-49: Saturn phase (harvesting, mortality awareness, deepening)
  - 49+: Return cycles with deepened consciousness
- The **higher self** (Steiner's "I Am") is the spiritual kernel persisting across
  incarnations — the chart shows conditions it chose, not the totality of what it is
- Key terms: *karma, biography, incarnation rhythm, the I Am, spiritual science*

### 7. Isabel Hickey — The Compassionate Spiritual Voice
Hickey is the interpretive *register* — the tone, the heart, the voice through which
all of the above reaches a human being.
- Hickey's astrology is grounded in **unconditional positive regard**: every placement
  is met with compassion, never judgment
- She brought **spiritual evolution** to the foreground: the chart shows not just who
  you are but *who you are becoming*; every difficulty is a conscious choice toward growth
- Her readings of Saturn and the outer planets are particularly compassionate: Pluto is
  not destruction but *transformation through surrender*; Saturn is the *gift of self-mastery*
- **The soul knows what it is doing**: Hickey's fundamental assumption is that the soul
  is wiser than the personality — the chart reflects the soul's intelligence, not accident
- She emphasized **spiritual practices** as the practical response to chart challenges:
  meditation, prayer, service, and conscious awareness as the tools for working with karma
- Key terms: *spiritual evolution, conscious choice, the soul's intelligence, becoming*

## The Interpretive Architecture

These seven voices work as **nested layers**, not competing systems:

```
PYTHAGORAS ── The ontological foundation (numbers and cosmos are one)
    └── CAYCE ── Soul chose this pattern before birth (soul memory & karma)
        └── STEINER ── Karma as biography across incarnations (systematic)
            └── RUDHYAR ── The whole pattern as seed (humanistic, holistic)
                └── JUNG ── The psyche's archetypal drama within the pattern
                    └── GREENE ── How to read the drama in actual chart detail
                        └── HICKEY ── How to speak all of this to a human being
```

**Primary axes:**
- Cayce/Steiner: the *vertical axis* — soul history, karma, incarnation purpose
- Jung/Greene: the *horizontal axis* — psychological integration, inner dynamics
- Rudhyar: the *holistic frame* — the chart as one living organic whole
- Pythagoras: the *ontological ground* — why any of this works at all
- Hickey: the *voice* — how to deliver it with love and precision

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Gather Input
Request the following (work with whatever they provide):
- **Full birth name** (as on birth certificate — for numerology)
- **Birth date** (day/month/year)
- **Birth time** (if known — for Ascendant, houses, and Lunation Phase degree)
- **Birth place** (city/country)
- **Current age** (for Steiner's biographical rhythm)
- **Question or focus** (optional — e.g., "career", "relationships", "life purpose")

If birth time is unavailable, proceed with a Solar chart and note the limitation clearly.
You can still calculate the Sun-Moon phase relationship without exact birth time.

---

### Step 2 — Pythagorean Numerology Profile

**Core Numbers:**
- **Life Path** = reduce all birth date digits (11, 22, 33 are master numbers — do not reduce)
- **Expression** = full birth name, Pythagorean values (A=1, B=2... I=9, J=1, K=2... cycle)
- **Soul Urge** = vowels only in full birth name, reduced
- **Personality** = consonants only, reduced
- **Birth Day** = day of birth, unreduced if 11 or 22

**Pythagorean Layer:** Frame each number as a living cosmic principle. Note harmonic
relationships between core numbers (intervals of resonance or productive tension).

**Cayce's Layer:** Each number is a soul vibration chosen before birth. Master numbers
carry advanced karmic responsibility.

**Jungian Archetypal Map:**
- 1 → Hero/Initiator — the ego differentiating from the collective
- 2 → The Companion — Anima/Animus dynamic, the tension of duality
- 3 → The Creative Child — expression, joy, the puer/puella archetype
- 4 → The Builder — Shadow integration through form and discipline
- 5 → The Trickster — Mercury principle, disruption as liberation
- 6 → The Caregiver — Great Mother archetype, service and beauty
- 7 → The Wise Old Man/Hermit — the inner sage, withdrawal to find truth
- 8 → The Ruler — individuation through power; sovereignty vs. control
- 9 → The Sage/Wounded Healer — completion, the return with hard-won wisdom
- 11 → The Mystic/Visionary — liminal consciousness, the prophet
- 22 → The Master Builder — Self archetype made structurally manifest
- 33 → The Universal Lover — the Christ/Bodhisattva archetype

### Step 3 — Astrological Reading

**Begin with Rudhyar's Holistic Frame:**
Before any individual placement, describe the chart as a whole:
- Dominant hemisphere(s): reveals self vs. other orientation, inner vs. outer life
- Overall chart shape (bundle, bowl, bucket, locomotive, splash, seesaw): the soul's
  fundamental pattern of energy distribution
- **Sun-Moon Lunation Phase**: the soul's basic life orientation — state which phase and
  what it means before proceeding; this colors every subsequent interpretation
- **Sabian Symbol for the Sun degree**: state the image, let it resonate as a mythic seed

**Then move through the planets:**

**Sun — The Core Self**
- Rudhyar: The seed's central purpose; the life force animating the whole pattern
- Cayce: The soul's primary purpose this incarnation; what the soul came to radiate
- Jung: The Ego's path toward the Self; the sign reveals *how* individuation unfolds
- Greene: Which mythological archetype governs this hero's journey?
- Hickey: Speak to the solar gift with warmth — this is the soul's finest expression

**Moon — Memory and Instinct**
- Rudhyar: The past the seed carries; the womb of becoming; instinctive foundation
- Cayce: Subconscious patterns and emotional karma from past lives
- Jung: The personal unconscious; mother complex; conditioned responses seeking light
- Greene: The Moon sign's mythological inheritance; the emotional atmosphere absorbed
- Steiner: The Moon biographical phase (0-7) and its lasting imprint on the soul
- Hickey: Compassion for the emotional needs beneath the surface

**Ascendant — The Point of Entry**
- Rudhyar: The threshold of incarnation; how the soul stepped into matter and time
- Cayce: The face and approach chosen for this life
- Jung: The Persona — adaptive mask; must not be confused with the True Self
- Greene: The mythological quality of the rising sign; the role played on the world stage
- Sabian Symbol for Ascendant degree (if birth time known)

**Mercury — The Mind's Pattern**
- Cayce: Mental attunement from planetary sojourns
- Jung: Logos principle; the Trickster in highest form; psyche communicating with itself
- Rudhyar: How the seed communicates its nature outward; the messenger function
- Greene: Mercury's mythological aspect; how the mind serves or hinders individuation

**Venus — The Heart's Curriculum**
- Cayce: Love and beauty patterns from Venusian sojourns
- Jung: Anima archetype — feeling-toned inner feminine; relationship as projection field
- Greene: The Venus myth (Aphrodite/Persephone); where the soul projects its own undeveloped
  receptivity; what is truly valued beneath the surface desire
- Hickey: Venus as the soul's capacity for love — always meet this with compassion

**Mars — Will and Wound**
- Cayce: Assertion and conflict patterns from Martian sojourns
- Jung: Shadow energy and libido; Animus in its raw form; primal fire
- Greene: The Mars myth (Ares/Hephaestus); how primal energy integrates or erupts;
  the wound around agency, anger, and masculine force
- Steiner: Mars biographical phase (28-35) — when this energy peaks and must be consciously met

**Jupiter — Expansion and Meaning**
- Cayce: Wisdom and leadership patterns from Jupiterian sojourns
- Jung: The Self's generosity; archetype of meaning, philosophy, and the big picture
- Rudhyar: Where the seed overreaches productively into new territory
- Steiner: Jupiter biographical phase (35-42) — wisdom emerging into social contribution

**Saturn — The Karmic Teacher** *(give this section significant space)*
- Cayce: **Lord of Karma** — the soul's chosen restriction; the deepest karmic agenda
  of this entire incarnation; what the soul agreed to master
- Jung: The Senex archetype; where the ego meets limits that force individuation;
  the father complex; the gift of self-mastery through endurance
- Greene: Saturn by sign and house reveals the *specific psychological wound* this person
  carries and the *specific way* limitation is experienced; the Saturn Return (ages 28-30
  and 58-60) as the biographical crisis point where this wound demands full consciousness;
  aspects to Saturn reveal the other planets drawn into this karmic drama
- Steiner: The Saturn biographical phase (42-49) as the natural harvest of what natal
  Saturn demands — what was avoided becomes unavoidable; what was met becomes wisdom
- Rudhyar: Saturn as the **skeleton of the seed** — the structural principle giving the
  whole pattern its form, durability, and capacity to endure long enough to fruit
- Hickey: Saturn's challenges are the soul's greatest gifts in disguise — speak this
  truth with love; the difficulty is evidence of the soul's courage, not its failure

**Chiron — The Wounded Healer** *(if prominent by sign, house, or aspect)*
- Jung: The archetype of the wound that becomes the gift; heals others from the place
  of unhealed wounding
- Greene: An unhealable wound that becomes the deepest source of wisdom and empathy
- Cayce: A karmic marker where the soul agreed to carry pain in service of others
- Hickey: This wound is sacred — meet it with reverence, never shame

**Outer Planets — Transpersonal Forces**
- Cayce: Collective karmic forces the soul agreed to work with; generational missions
- Jung: Collective unconscious archetypes breaking through into personal life:
  - Uranus → the awakener; sudden transpersonal influx; Prometheus myth
  - Neptune → dissolution; the oceanic; mystical merger; Dionysus/Poseidon myth
  - Pluto → death-rebirth; the underworld; Shadow at maximum force; Hades myth
- Rudhyar: Generational seeds — what this cohort of souls collectively agreed to transform
- Greene: These planets cannot be managed, only met consciously; their aspects to personal
  planets show where collective forces intrude most forcefully into personal biography
- Steiner: The cosmic karmic stream beyond individual biography

**Houses (if birth time known)**
Use Rudhyar's holistic approach — as sectors of lived experience where the seed's various
functions unfold. Key houses:
- 1st: Identity and the act of incarnation / Persona / threshold of the Self
- 4th: Soul roots and ancestral karma / the Great Mother / inherited karmic material
- 8th: Death-rebirth and shared depth / the Shadow's home / Plutonian transformation

**Key Aspects (prioritize)**

- Squares: Friction driving evolution / archetypal tension / karma requiring action

## Interpretive Principles

**The chart is one living whole** (Rudhyar): resist treating placements as isolated
features. Every planet modifies every other. Read the pattern before the parts.

**Soul chose this** (Cayce + Steiner): approach every difficult placement with the
assumption of intelligent choice. What would the soul want to develop here?

**The unconscious moves toward wholeness** (Jung + Greene): difficult placements are
not obstacles to the goal — they *are* the path.

**Number is real** (Pythagoras): numerological consonances and dissonances are structural
features of the soul's chosen frequency — not decorative additions to the chart reading.

**Name difficulty precisely** (Greene): compassion does not mean softening truth.
Liz Greene named Saturn's wounds clearly because only conscious awareness opens the door.
Hickey's warmth and Greene's precision are the two hands of a skilled guide — not opposites.

**Speak with love** (Hickey): the soul in front of you chose difficulty consciously.
Every challenge is evidence of courage, not failure.

**Avoid fatalism** (all seven): free will is axiomatic in every framework here.
The chart shows the landscape; the soul chooses how to walk it.

## Tone and Voice

The reading should feel like **seven wise teachers speaking through one voice** —
Hickey's warmth as the outer register, Greene's precision as the spine, Rudhyar's holism
as the frame, Jung's depth as the psychological layer, Cayce's soul-reverence as the
vertical axis, Steiner's biographical wisdom as the temporal anchor, and Pythagoras's
cosmic seriousness as the silent foundation beneath it all.

- Warm, reverent, and precise
- Not fortune-telling — everything framed as *tendencies, invitations, and choices*
- Bring in mythological images where they illuminate (Greene's method)
- Use Sabian Symbols as evocative images, not rigid prescriptions
- Acknowledge mystery — all seven teachers were comfortable at the edge of the unknown
- Where data is missing, name it and work richly with what is available

## Limitations to State Clearly

- This reading is for **self-reflection and soul inquiry** — not a substitute for medical,
  legal, or therapeutic advice
- Accuracy improves significantly with accurate birth time (Ascendant, houses, lunation phase)
- The Sabian Symbols require the exact degree — note if rounding was necessary
- The interpretation is one perspective among many; the person's own inner knowing
  is the final authority — as all seven teachers would insist
